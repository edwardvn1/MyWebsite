My Educational Philosophy
I'm a strong proponent of constructivism and social constructivism in the classroom. At times it seems that this pedagogical approach clashes with the typical formal education setup. Students are often expected to fit a very specific mold which often leads to atypical students being left behind. Considering class sizes it is also challenging to cater to individual learning styles.
With mathematics, however, several learning styles can effectively be combined since math lends itself so well to auditory and visual learning. My expertise in sign language also gives me a deeper appreciation for the spatial aspect of learning which often comes in handy when teaching various concepts.

Critical Thinking and Metacognition
As a level 3 practitioner of the Feuerstein Instrumental Enrichment model, I found that critical thinking and metacognition are critical for students to identify their own learning styles. By guiding students to identify their own obstacles and thinking strategies, they become more and more suited to the mathematical and logical thinking patterns required for mathematics and programming. The link between math and coding is also mutually beneficial since the theories behind computational thinking can effectively be applied to mathematical thinking as well.
